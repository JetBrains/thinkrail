Devin's model: the unit of the UI is a session — the chat plus that agent's own workbench. Shell, editor, plan and diffs are per-agent surfaces attached to the conversation, not global panels. You spectate the desk; you can take any surface over.
  1. Top bar = session switcher. Sessions are the app's top-level tabs, floating in the 34px bar itself — no dead chrome.
  2. Side-by-side agents. Each session is one full screen; ◫ opens two sessions next to each other — grids come from splitting sessions, not center tabs.
  3. No tree panel at all. The project is a pill; each session carries its own worktree (⌥ main). Nothing idles at 18% width.
  4. Per-agent shell. Every agent brings its own terminal — peeked here, full desk height when its tab is active.
  5. Files live with the work. Editor/Changes sit on the agent's desk, always one tab away from the conversation they belong to.
  6. Uniform, splittable surfaces. Plan/Editor/Shell/Changes are swappable desk tabs — drag one to a half to split the desk.
