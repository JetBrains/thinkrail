rookery

Help casual birders log a sighting in under 10 seconds — the fastest, friendliest birding log.

Context

Casual birders today log sightings in Notes, eBird, or Merlin. Notes loses entries in a sea of grocery lists; eBird and Merlin assume you're a scientist or a competitive lister. None of them respect the moment of seeing the bird — the friction kills the joy of birding.

Problem

Logging requires both hands, slow forms, and signal. By the time you're done the bird's gone. The hobbyist who wants to remember, not document, gets punished. They abandon and go back to a half-filled list in Notes.

Scope

In v1 1 of 4 added to board
  • • One-tap log — photo + auto-location + species guess. ≤10s by default.
  • • Offline-first — every action works without signal; sync later.
  • • Personal log view — chronological, map, life-list count.
  • • Edit-after — fill in details days later if you want.

Out of scope (explicitly not v1)

  • Social feeds, follows, comments — you're logging for yourself.
  • Audio/photo ID — we lean on a third-party service, no in-house ML.
  • Trip planning, hotspot maps, rare-bird alerts.

Requirements

Must have

#RequirementRationale
1Log in <10s with one thumb, no signal.The single goal of the product.
2Sightings persist locally first; sync is a background concern.You log in the field; signal is unreliable.
3Location optional & private by default; never leaves the device unless explicitly shared.Birders are protective of rare-species locations.

Nice to have

#RequirementRationale
1Auto life-list count.Cheap dopamine for the hobbyist.
2Export to CSV / GeoJSON.Trust: your data is yours.

Constraints

  • Mobile-first (React Native / Expo). Desktop is read-only.
  • Cheap to host: no realtime, no per-user backend cost beyond storage.
  • No PII in logs; sightings are private until shared.
